A Brief History

Education and Career

  • 1933 - Born on March 15th in Brooklyn, New York

  • 1954 - Graduated top of her class from Cornell University. Married Martin D. Ginsburg, her lifelong husband and cheerleader, the same year.

  • 1959 - With a class of over 500 students, she was one of the eight women attending Harvard Law School and became the first woman to act as a memebr of the prestigious Harvard Law Review. She later transferred to Columbia University where she graduated first in her class.

  • 1970s - Ginsberg became the first female tenured professor at Columbia. In addition, she acted as the director of the Women's Rights Project of the American Civil Liberties Union, arguing six milestone cases on gender eqaulity in the U.S. Supreme Court.

  • 1980 - Appointed to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia.

  • 1993 - Appointed to the U.S. Supreme Court.

Notorious Cases as a Supreme Court Justice

  • 1996 - Wrote the Supreme Court's landmark decision in United States v. Virginia, which held that the state-supported Virginia Military Institute could not refuse to admit women.

  • 2000 - In the presidential election between George W. Bush and Al Gore, Ginsbgerg objected the court's majority opinion favoring Bush, coining the term "I dissent" and diverting from the tradition of including "respectfully" at the end.

  • 2007 - Gave a dissenting opion in the Gonzales v. Carhart case which upheld the Partial-Birth Abortion Ban Act of 2003.

  • 2015 - In King v.BurwellGinsburg sided with the majority decision to uphold a key componenet to the 2010 Affordable Care Act. That same year, the ruling on Obergefell v. Hodges made same sex marriage legal in all 50 states.
